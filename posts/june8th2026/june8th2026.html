Since We've Arrived...

Hello all! After our northernly drive up (in the car we were blessed with being able to acquire the day before we left!) we spent a week at cousin Claire's apartment which was a huge blessing! We slept basically a full day to recover from the trip (Kaden drove literally the whole way except when we went through Chicago which is INSANE smooch smooch) Kaden then began his internship at Northwestern University! He can explain the details better than I ever could but he's been doing his own research of sorts and getting to work with robot arms! I spent the week reading like a fiend, cleaning, going on walks by a park close by, and making food! We went on several walks together at the River park, relaxing, and being together :heart: Then memorial weekend came! Claire and Austin flew in and together we all joined cousin Julianne and "curious" George (ok we don't have to put that in i just did it cause the "C in cousin") at a VERBO! We laughed making jokes, catching up on years apart, and chatted together around a delicious meal of salmon and green beans. We played a super fun but stressful game called "The Farmer Game" where you basically are a legit farmer and acquire an absurd amount of crippling debt. In the morning we were joined by Todd and Laura Claire and Julianne's parents! After eggs and bacon we packed up a pic-nic lunch and headed for the Line train and Chicago! Claire led the way to Union Station where we saw the 100 year old train station and a statue of a noble woman holding all things a chicken. Then Claire showed us where she works! Very clean marble, high marbled walls and rows of crisp shiny computers. We decided to have our tasty lunch in the kitchen area (alas white monsters lured and captured the Triguerio boys) and talked and laughed some more. We walked around the city Claire pointing out the building and memorable spots till we made it to the Architecture boat tour on the Chicago river! It was a stunning experience! Our tour guide, Mark, educated us on the history of the buildings that lined the river, recounting who built them, how they were used, the age, and style in which they were designed. A green bottle of a tower built during prohibition, a cluster of skyscrapers looking like a waterfall, and of course the Sears tower which used to be the tallest building in America. The views were astounding! After the tour we stopped quickly at an adjoining museum, we walked through a beautifully decorated building called the Cultural Arts Center where a wedding was taking place. Then we headed back to eat some dinner at a divine Columbian restaurant where the walls were covered entirely of pennies. Unfortunately we did not salsa (as Holly requested us to do), but we talked and had a dandy time together. A woman took photos of us and brought us framed pictures of us that she had taken and Todd bought one. Then we went back to the VERBO and after some chit-chatting, retired to snooze.
