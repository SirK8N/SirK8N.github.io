Abstract (a summary in plain language)

This blog post is formatted similar to a typical research paper. The feeling of looking at a dense scientific paper for the first time is confusing and overwhelming, filled with buzz words and academic terminology crafted to sound as smart as possible. I imagine that same feeling is the same as whenever I try to summarize what I do for work. In this post I’m going to both explain how to read and digest a research paper AND what I’ll be doing this summer for my internship. Hopefully by the end you’ll have a better grasp on both.

1. Introduction

My last two summers were spent working as an undergraduate researcher. In Summer 2025 (S25) I attened a professional development session led by Priyam Mazumdar that discussed how to read and digest a research paper. The technique he shared was introduced as the "Three Pass Method" introduced by Computer Scientist Srinivasan Keshav. This process is a structured approach to analyzing and reading dense information. I've been using this process during my summer 2026 (S26) researching at Northwestern University.

2. Methodology

The Three Pass Method (3PM) has (unsuprisingly) three stages of reading. This section outlines each stage.

2.1 First Pass: Overview

"The first pass is a quick scan to get a bird’s-eye view of the paper. You can also decide whether you need to do any more passes. This pass should take about five to ten minutes"
Essentially skim the paper lol. I should say that if you're trying to read anything with a narrative structure, like edu-tainment or regular entertainment. Before reading any more of this section, pause- read the headers and skim the material of the rest of this blog post. I've included some of more complicated aspects in the later sections to make certain parts harder to spoil.
