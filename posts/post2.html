Recipes, rhythms, and shared stories

She cooked, I listened, and we wrote about the small, warm habits that make our days feel grounded and bright.

There's a rhythm to our kitchen on Sunday afternoons—the sound of the knife hitting the cutting board, the gentle sizzle of herbs hitting hot oil, the quiet playlist in the background. These are the moments where our life feels most clear and most real.

Cooking together isn't just about the food. It's about creating a space where we can talk, think, and be present with each other. The recipes are just an excuse to practice being intentional about time spent together.

We've started documenting our favorite recipes and the stories behind them. Each one carries a memory—where we were, who we were with, what we learned that day.

📺 Things we're watching

  • Cooking shows that focus on technique and tradition rather than speed
  • Films about family and connection, particularly those with food scenes
  • Food documentaries exploring how culture shapes cuisine

💝 Things we recommend

  • Fresh herbs from the farmers market - the difference is remarkable
  • Handwritten recipe journals to capture memories alongside ingredients
  • Cooking without rushing - put your phone away and be present
  • Trying a recipe new to you each week, even if it feels challenging
